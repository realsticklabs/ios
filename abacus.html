Abacuses

History

Abacuses have been used for 4000 years in many parts of the world including many Eurasian Countries. The exact location of where it was made is unknown with some fighting that people think its been originated of the following countries, China, Babylon, Greece and others.

Types

The modern abacus is usually made of wood or plastic and is a rectangular box with nine vertical rods and beads. The beads above the horizontal crossbar are called Heaven beads, and the beads below the bar are called earth beads.

Uses

Abacuses are still used today in accounting, education and in austere environments. It can help children develop better comprehension skills, agile motor skills, and confidence.

Other Names

The modern Japanese abacus is called a Soroban. In China it is called a Suanpan.
